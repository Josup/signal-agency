The
Problem.

A patient in Park Slope opens ChatGPT and types "best optometrist near me in Brooklyn." Three names appear. Yours isn't one of them. She calls the first one.

¹By the time you read this, that conversation has happened roughly forty times today inside ten blocks of your storefront. Multiply it by every "near me" question your customers ask: best dentist, best gym, best private BJJ instructor, best frame shop. The new local search isn't a map — it's a sentence, and someone is being named in it.

²The names AI offers aren't random. They go to businesses with structured data, consistent citations, real reviews, and content written for retrieval. Most local sites have none of these. Signal exists for the businesses who would have shown up — if only the machine could read them.

How it
works.

Three moves, in order. Same as it ever was, only the index has changed.

01.

Rebuild the site.

A fast, structured Next.js build. Static where it can be, server-rendered where it should be. Every page exists at a clean, retrievable URL with one job, written in the language a customer actually uses.

Deliverable~3 weeks
02.

Add the markup.

Schema.org / JSON-LD for LocalBusiness, FAQ, Product, Service, BreadcrumbList. Geo coordinates, hours, payment, and entity links the AI can verify. The page now says what it is, not just what it looks like.

Deliverable~1 week
03.

Publish content AI cites.

Weekly long-form posts written for retrieval — neighborhood guides, comparison pages, "near me" answers, FAQ depth. Each post is monitored across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI, Claude. Cited posts get reinforced, dead ones get rewritten.
