# How to Write a Neighborhood Landing Page That Gets Cited by AI

A neighborhood landing page gets cited by AI when it answers a specific local query with named entities, structured facts, and a clear geographic signal. Generic location pages with placeholder copy get ignored. Specific, factual, retrieval-grade pages get pulled into ChatGPT responses, Perplexity answers, and Google AI Overviews.

Most Brooklyn businesses have location pages. Almost none are written for AI retrieval. The difference is not design. It's structure and specificity.

## Why Generic Pages Fail the AI Citation Test

AI systems pull from sources they can quote with confidence. Vague language fails that test. Phrases like "proudly serving the Crown Heights community" or "your local experts in Williamsburg" give an AI model nothing to work with. There's no fact to cite. No entity to anchor. No answer to a real query.

We ran 240 prompts across ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews targeting Brooklyn service businesses. Pages that contained a business name, a neighborhood name, a specific service description, and at least one verifiable fact got cited in AI responses at roughly 3x the rate of pages that contained only marketing copy. The specificity threshold matters more than page length.

## The Five Structural Elements That Drive Citations

Every neighborhood landing page we build for Signal clients includes these five elements in this order.

**1. A direct answer in the first 40 words.** State what the business is, where it is, and what it does. "Nostrand Optical is a full-scope optometry practice on Nostrand Avenue in Crown Heights, Brooklyn. They offer eye exams, contact lens fittings, and same-day frames." That sentence is citable. An AI can pull it verbatim.

**2. A neighborhood-specific context block.** Name the neighborhood. Name adjacent blocks or landmarks when relevant. "Crown Heights sits between Eastern Parkway and Empire Boulevard, serving a dense residential population with limited walkable eyecare options." This anchors the page to a real place and signals geographic relevance to retrieval systems.

**3. Factual service details with specifics.** Hours, pricing ranges, appointment types, insurance accepted, turnaround times. Any fact an AI can use to answer a user query. Nostrand Optical's page lists that they accept VSP and EyeMed — two of the most-searched insurance terms in Brooklyn eyecare queries. That detail appears in AI responses to "optometrist Crown Heights takes VSP."

**4. A named practitioner or operator.** Solo operators and small practices that include the owner's name, credentials, and role get cited as authoritative sources at a higher rate than anonymous practice pages. The Person entity matters. A page that names "Dr. [Name], OD, practicing optometry in Crown Heights since 2019" gives AI something to attribute.

**5. A clear geographic boundary statement.** "Located at [address], serving Crown Heights, Prospect Heights, and Bed-Stuy." This expands the retrieval surface without diluting the primary neighborhood signal. AI systems use geographic radius logic. Give them the boundaries explicitly.

## The Prompt Vocabulary Your Page Needs to Match

AI search queries for local businesses follow recognizable patterns. Users don't search "optometrist Brooklyn" the way they used to. They ask "who's the best optometrist in Crown Heights that takes VSP" or "where can I get glasses same day in Brooklyn."

Your landing page needs to contain the vocabulary of those queries. Not as keyword stuffing. As direct answers.

Build a list of ten questions a new customer would ask before choosing your business. Answer each one in a single sentence. Put those answers on the page. This is not a new SEO idea. It's a retrieval instruction. AI systems scan for question-answer pairs because users phrase queries as questions.

The pages that fail this test read like brochures. The pages that pass it read like a knowledgeable staff member answering a phone call.

## Publish Cadence Compounds the Effect

A single well-structured neighborhood page is a starting point. The signal strengthens when it's supported by consistent content. We track citation frequency across our Brooklyn clients against their content cadence. Clients publishing four or more pieces per week see AI citation rates improve by roughly 60% within 90 days compared to clients publishing once a month.

Nostrand Optical runs four SEO posts per week, all tied to Crown Heights and the surrounding neighborhoods. Each post adds a retrieval surface. Each surface reinforces the primary neighborhood page. The cumulative effect is that AI systems treat the site as an authoritative local source, not a one-off result.

If your neighborhood page is the only content on the site touching your location, you're relying on one data point to establish authority. AI systems weight sources with consistent, verifiable, location-specific content higher than isolated pages.
