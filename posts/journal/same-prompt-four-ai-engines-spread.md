# We Ran the Same Prompt on ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI, and Gemini: Here Is the Spread

Four AI engines. One prompt. Four completely different answers. The business that ranked first in one engine didn't appear at all in two others.

We ran this test across 12 Brooklyn clients over six weeks in Q1 2026. The variation wasn't minor. It was structural. Each engine pulls from different source types, weights different signals, and has a different threshold for what counts as a citable local business. If you're optimizing for one engine and ignoring the other three, you're invisible to most AI-driven discovery.

## The Prompt and the Method

We used a single prompt template: "Who are the best [service type] in [Brooklyn neighborhood]?" We ran it against ChatGPT (GPT-4o), Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, and Gemini Advanced. Same prompt. Same time of day. Logged over 60 individual query sessions across six service categories: optometry, martial arts, barbershops, independent retail, physical therapy, and restaurants.

We recorded which businesses were cited by name, which sources each engine referenced, and whether any Signal clients appeared in the response. We did not prompt with business names. We did not prime the engines. Clean queries every time.

## ChatGPT Cited Fewer Businesses but Held Positions Longer

ChatGPT returned the shortest citation lists. Across our 60 queries, it named an average of 2.8 businesses per response. Perplexity named 5.1. But ChatGPT's citations were more stable. When a business appeared in a ChatGPT response, it appeared consistently across repeated queries over a four-week window. Position drift was low.

Brooklyn BJJ Lessons held the top citation slot for "BJJ private lessons Brooklyn" across 22 consecutive ChatGPT queries over 31 days. That result first appeared 41 days after launch. Once ChatGPT locked it in, it stayed locked. The mechanism appears to be retrieval confidence. ChatGPT cites businesses it can corroborate across multiple structured sources. Thin citations or inconsistent NAP data produced zero appearances in our test set.

## Perplexity Pulled from Live Web Sources and Rewarded Recency

Perplexity cited the most businesses per query and was the most volatile. A business that appeared in a Tuesday query sometimes dropped out by Thursday if a competitor published new content. Perplexity is actively crawling. It rewards freshness in a way the other three engines don't.

Across our test set, 68% of Perplexity citations linked directly to a web source published within the prior 90 days. That means a business with no content published recently had a measurably lower chance of appearing, even if it had strong historical authority. For our clients running four posts per week, Perplexity was the most responsive engine. It noticed new content within seven to ten days of publication consistently.

## Google AI Overviews Demanded Structured Signals First

Google AI Overviews was the most demanding engine in our test. It cited businesses at a lower rate than Perplexity and was harder to break into cold. But when a business appeared in an AI Overview, it also tended to appear in the map pack and organic results simultaneously. The lift was compounded.

Nostrand Optical appeared in AI Overview responses for "optometrist Crown Heights" within three weeks of launch. That same period, they held four rich results on Google. The pattern held across other clients: rich results and AI Overview citations moved together. Google appears to use structured data signals as a prerequisite before surfacing a local business in generated responses. Businesses without structured signals simply didn't appear in Overviews in our test, regardless of review count or domain age.

## Gemini Leaned on Google Business Profile Data Above Everything Else

Gemini's citations correlated most strongly with Google Business Profile completeness. In 74% of cases where a business appeared in a Gemini response, that business had a verified GBP with at least eight populated fields, a primary category match, and reviews posted within the prior 60 days.

Gemini also showed the highest rate of citing businesses that were not on the first page of traditional organic results. A well-optimized GBP could get a business into Gemini responses even when its website had weak SEO. This makes Gemini the most accessible engine for brick-and-mortar businesses with limited web presence, but it also means GBP neglect is the single fastest way to disappear from Gemini entirely.

## What the Spread Means for Your Optimization Strategy

No single optimization covers all four engines. Here is what moves the needle on each:

- **ChatGPT:** Structured data, consistent citations across directories, corroborated NAP. Stability over recency.
- **Perplexity:** Fresh content. Four posts per week outperforms four posts per month by a factor we measured at roughly 3x citation frequency.
- **Google AI Overviews:** Structured signals are the gate. Rich results and Overviews are not separate goals. They're the same goal.
- **Gemini:** GBP completeness. Category accuracy. Recent reviews. The website matters less here than anywhere else.

Businesses that appeared across all four engines in our test shared three traits: verified GBP with eight or more populated fields, structured data on their website, and at least two pieces of new content published per month. No client with all three was absent from all four engines. Every client missing two or more of the three was absent from at least three engines.
